Illusion of "knowing" about the customer and the market

Case Study: Sprint

Sprint was an app for trainers, using ai to generate content for their customers. It was at first positively received, and generated revenue. But eventually they shut down. Why?

  1. Trainers were not familiar with digital devices(computers). They do everything on mobile.
  2. They are very busy, and their future consideration extended only to the next month.
  3. Pushing for them to use them by appealing to reason, failed. Spectacularly.

Do not try to change your customers work patterns.

Do not try to teach them anything

Trainers are poor, are not serious about their work.

Do not invest into poor man's market.

No return customers.

Markets.

Korea is too small

30000 trainers. 100 used it. Not even remotely feasible.

Market maturity

The trainers are not (yet) interested in ai markets.

The service may be too sophisticated for current customers, or vice versa.
